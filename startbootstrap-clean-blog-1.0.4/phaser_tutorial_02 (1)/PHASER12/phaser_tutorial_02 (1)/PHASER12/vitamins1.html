Vitamins

VitaminWhat they doFoods that have them
Vitamin AMakes your eyes, teeth and skin healthy. Milk, eggs, butter(small amounts),cheese, yogurt, dark colored vegetables
Vitamin BHelps the body use food as energy, and helps digest proteins.Whole grains, legumes(beans), lean meat, and fish
Vitamin CThis is an antioxidant that helps the immune system and helps your body absorb iron.Citrus foods(orange, lemon), strawberries, spinach, tomatoes
Vitamin DThis is also called the "sunshine vitamin" because you can get vitamin D from going outside in the sun. Getting vitamin D helps absorb calcium for healthy bones and teethDairy, fatty fish(salmon)
Vitamin KThis makes you blood clot(important for when you get a cut and bleed).Cauliflower, cabbage, dark leafy vegetables(kale, spinach, collard greens)
CalciumCalcium helps your body build strong bones and teeth. It also clots your blood when needed, can squeeze and relax your muscles, and helps with having a normal heart beat.Green leafy vegetables, salmon and sardines or any fish with soft bones as well as nuts such as brazil nuts, and sunflower seeds. Let's not forget the most common sources of calcium - dairy, cheese, and yogurt are good as well as milk, which helps your body absorb calcium.
MagnesiumYou need magnesium for healthy bones, teeth, and to energize you metabolism as well as balance acid in your body.Milk,grains,and lean meat.
PotassiumThis keeps a normal water balance between our cells and body as well as control muscles contractions(So next time you have cramps a lot, eat something with tons of potassium like bananas).Dark leafy greens, white beans, bananas of course, avocado, fish, such as salmon, and mushrooms.
IronIron is needed for our bodies to for haemoglobin which carries oxygen, the stuff we breathe from our lungs to the cells in our body.Meat, eggs, green leafy vegetables and legumes.
ZincNeeded so our cells can multiply. Also, this helps our tissues and muscles grow.Meat, seafood, liver, eggs,milk, and whole-grains.
